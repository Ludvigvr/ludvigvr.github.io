Ancestor worship

Vietnamese ancestor worship is characterized by a great reverence for forefathers and their lasting impact. It is influenced by both Confucian and Taoist principles of philosophy, emphasizing family unity and veneration of elders. Families usually maintain sacred altars devoted to ancestors and participate in holy rituals on special occasions.

These ceremonies include burning of incense and reciting prayers, which symbolize a connection between worlds. Places designated to Đình's, such as the Đình, commemorate local heroes and ancestors, contributing to social cohesion. The spirits of ancestors are believed to influence daily life, and adhering to their guidance is essential. The practice of ancestor worship is still widespread, reflecting the significance of Vietnamese heritage and familial ties amidst societal changes.

Visiting the pagoda by the beginning of year.

Visiting pagodas during Tet, the Lunar New Year, is a beloved tradition in Vietnam. People nationwide flock to pagodas to pray and seek blessings for the upcoming year, as they hold great significance during this time. Pagodas serve as places of worship and spiritual reflection, where individuals pay respects to the Buddha, seek guidance from spiritual figures, and honor their ancestors' protection and blessings. Upon arrival, visitors light incense, offer symbolic items, and engage in rituals like bowing and reciting prayers. This practice is not just spiritual but also social, as families and friends come together to pay respects and strengthen bonds. The annual pilgrimage embodies Vietnamese spirituality, cultural traditions, and the belief in prayers and blessings, reminding us of the importance of spiritual well-being and a prosperous year.

God of Wealth Worship in business

In Vietnamese business culture, the god of wealth, known as "Ông Táo" or "Thần Tài," is held in high esteem for his reputed ability to grant wealth to those who seek his assistance. Businesspeople often display portraits or images of this celebrated deity during significant occasions, believing that his presence will bring them success and prosperity. To invoke the blessings of the god of wealth, entrepreneurs engage in a range of offerings and rituals like burning incense, reciting prayers, and making specific requests. These practices are believed to attract good luck and fortune, and connect businesspeople to the spiritual realm. During Tet, the Vietnamese Lunar New Year, businesses host elaborate festivities adorned with red and gold decorations. The color red symbolizes good luck and prosperity while gold represents wealth and abundance. By creating an environment steeped in these auspicious colors, businesses hope to attract positive energy and financial success throughout the upcoming year.

The exchange of lucky red envelopes (li xi) is another captivating tradition associated with the god of wealth. These envelopes contain monetary gifts and are given during festive occasions, particularly during Tet. Gifting red envelopes is believed to bestow good fortune and blessings upon both the giver and the recipient, reflecting the Vietnamese belief in the interconnectedness of spirituality and material wealth. Overall, the veneration of the god of wealth in Vietnamese business culture reflects the profound belief of Vietnamese people in seeking divine intervention and guidance to achieve prosperity and financial success. The display of portraits, the performance of rituals, and the exchange of red envelopes all contribute to cultivating an environment that is spiritually charged and conducive to attracting wealth and good fortune in the realm of business.
